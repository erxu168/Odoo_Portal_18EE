# Product Marketing Context — What a Jerk

*Last updated: 2026-04-23*

---

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Berlin's first proper Jamaican quick-service spot on Kottbusser Damm — real jerk, real heat, fresh from the counter.

**What it does:** What a Jerk serves authentic Jamaican food — jerk chicken wraps, patties, curry dishes, oxtail stew, brown chicken stew, jackfruit stew, fried chicken — with real scotch-bonnet heat (adjustable on request). Quick service counter for dine-in and takeaway, plus delivery on Wolt and Uber Eats. Late-night until 1am Fri/Sat.

**Product category:** Jamaican / Caribbean quick-service restaurant (QSR)

**Product type:** Physical restaurant + delivery

**Business model:** Counter service + takeaway + delivery (Wolt, Uber Eats)

**Pricing:**
- Wraps: €8.50
- Dishes: €12–15
- Sides: festival, coleslaw, escovitch-pickled vegetables

**Location:** Kottbusser Damm 96, 10967 Berlin (Kreuzberg/Neukölln border, near Hermannplatz)

**Opening:** ~Mid-May 2026 (3 weeks from 2026-04-23)

**Hours:** Mon–Thu + Sun 12:00–23:00 / Fri–Sat 12:00–01:00

## Target Audience

**Target customers:** Berlin creative/professional class, 25–40, smart-casual, adventurous palate, English-speaking, disposable income. Wears COS / Acne. Works in IT, sales, medical. Well-traveled, follows food trends, foodie-adjacent.

**Three customer modes:**
1. **Lunch crowd** — workers from nearby offices, students, locals grabbing something interesting instead of another döner
2. **Late-night crowd** — bar / night-out groups on Fri/Sat until 1am, looking for food that's more exciting than a kebab
3. **Delivery crowd** — work-from-home, couples, curious-but-lazy, ordering via Wolt/Uber Eats

**Primary use case:** "I'm hungry on Kottbusser Damm and want something interesting, not the same thing I've had 100 times."

**Jobs to be done:**
- Break out of the döner/pizza/Vietnamese rotation
- Satisfy a craving for bold, spicy, unfamiliar food
- Grab something filling and fast without sitting down
- Share a new find with friends (Instagram-worthy)

**Use cases / scenarios:**
- Quick weekday lunch between meetings
- Post-drinks food after a Neukölln bar crawl
- Weekend couple dinner on Wolt
- Showing a visiting friend "something you've never tried"

**Dream customer:** Lives within 10 minutes of Kottbusser Damm, handles heat, comes every week, doesn't complain about price.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Kottbusser Damm is saturated with mediocre döner, pizza, burger, and Vietnamese spots. The dream customer — well-traveled, curious, foodie — is bored and has no Jamaican option in the neighborhood.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Döner: repetitive, no surprise left
- Pizza / burger: basic, nothing new
- Vietnamese: decent but already well-explored
- Existing Jamaican spots in Berlin are across town (Moabit, different pockets of Neukölln) or event-based (Good Vybz)

**What it costs them:** Settling for "fine" lunches and nights out instead of memorable ones. Missing out on the trend of trying real Caribbean food.

**Emotional tension:** "I want adventure but I don't want to travel across town. I want heat but I'm worried it'll be too much. I want something new but I'm tired of disappointing openings."

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct (other Jamaican in Berlin):**
- **Sweet Jamaica Food** (Neukölln, different pocket) — delivery/pickup only, not on Kottbusser Damm
- **Rosa Caleta** (Kreuzberg) — sit-down fusion, different segment (table service, evening)
- **Good Vybz** (Kreuzberg, gymnastics club) — event-driven, not everyday QSR
- **Ya-Man** (Moabit) — far across town
- **Royal Jerk, Sweet Jamaica Jerk** — food trucks at Tempelhof / Kulturbrauerei / Teufelsberg, not neighborhood-based
- → **None are on Kottbusser Damm. None are true neighborhood QSR in Neukölln.**

**Secondary (QSR competitors on Kottbusser Damm — the real daily fight):**
- Mardin, Gokoreç, Imren, Gel Gör (Turkish)
- Kotti Dang, Con Tho (Vietnamese)
- Ssam (Korean — owned by same operator)
- Pizza and burger spots
- → These are where customers go *by default*. What a Jerk has to break the habit.

**Indirect:**
- Cooking at home
- Späti snacks after drinks
- Meal prep / delivery subscriptions

**How they fall short:** None of them offer Caribbean flavor, real heat, or the novelty of something Berlin hasn't been saturated with yet.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- The **only proper Jamaican QSR on Kottbusser Damm** (and one of very few in all of Berlin)
- **Real scotch-bonnet heat** — not dialed down for Berlin palates, but optional for those who can't handle it
- **Operator pedigree** — same team as Ssam (Korean BBQ next door), proven on this exact street
- **Researched abroad** — years of eating through Vancouver and Toronto's Jamaican scenes (the two Jamaican food capitals of North America) before bringing it home
- **Signature items**: jerk chicken wrap, jerk chicken dish, Jamaican patties
- **Late-night Fri/Sat until 1am** — directly targets the post-bar hunger window
- **Bright, playful, Caribbean-vibe space** with heavy attention to detail — designed to be shareable on Instagram

**How we do it differently:** Bring Jamaican food to where the hungry are, instead of making them travel. Keep it fast, filling, and affordable (€8.50 wraps) while respecting the heat and flavor profile that makes the cuisine special.

**Why it's better:** You can walk up, try something genuinely new to Berlin, not have to commit to a sit-down meal, and get out in under ten minutes with food that'll ruin döner for you.

**Why customers choose us:** Novelty + quality + location + operator trust. They already walk past this corner every day.

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I don't know what Jamaican food is / I won't like it" | Start simple: "Try the jerk wrap — €8.50, it's smoky grilled chicken in a soft wrap, like nothing else on this street." Offer a visual menu, clear descriptions in plain English/German, avoid jargon like "escovitch" without translation. |
| "Too spicy for me" | Lead with: "You choose your heat." Scotch-bonnet is the default, but we dial it down on request. Always mark spice levels on the menu. |
| "€15 is expensive for a quick lunch" | Reframe: wraps start at €8.50 (competitive with a good döner). €12–15 is a full dish with meat, sides, and rice — portion and quality justify it. Never apologize for price; lead with what's on the plate. |

**Anti-persona (who we are NOT for):**
- People who can't or won't eat spicy food
- Price-complainers who want under €8 meals
- People who want familiar German / Turkish comfort food
- Anyone looking for a sit-down fine dining experience

## Customer Language

**How they describe the problem:**
- "I'm so sick of döner"
- "Everything on this street is mid"
- "I want to try something new"
- "There's nothing good near me anymore"

**How they'll describe us (aspirational):**
- "There's this new Jamaican spot on Kotti Damm, it's actually insane"
- "You have to try the jerk wrap"
- "The heat is real but you can pick how spicy"
- "It's from the Ssam guys"

**Words to use:**
- Jerk, smoky, fire, bold, fresh, filling, playful, warm
- "Ya mon", "one love", "irie" — used tastefully, not forced
- Heat levels: "mild / medium / jerk / full fire" (or similar tiered naming)

**Words to avoid:**
- "Ethnic", "exotic" (dated, othering)
- Overly corporate or sanitized language
- German-only formality (feels out of place for the vibe)
- Over-claiming authenticity the owner can't back up (e.g. "taste of my grandmother's kitchen")

**Language mix:** Bilingual — German + English. English is the default for social / Instagram (Berlin creative class speaks English); German is important for menu, signage, older walk-ins, and Wolt listings.

**Glossary:**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Jerk | Jamaican marinade + grilling method; allspice, scotch bonnet, thyme, smoke |
| Scotch bonnet | The signature Jamaican chili — fruity, floral, very hot |
| Festival | Sweet fried cornmeal dumpling, classic side |
| Escovitch | Pickled vegetable relish (onion, carrot, bell pepper, vinegar, scotch bonnet) |
| Patty | Flaky yellow pastry stuffed with seasoned meat or veg |
| Oxtail stew | Braised oxtail with butter beans, thyme, allspice |
| Brown chicken stew | Slow-cooked chicken in caramelized-sugar gravy |

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Playful, welcoming, cool.

**Style:** Conversational, bilingual, confident but not cocky. Uses Jamaican phrases ("ya mon", "one love") tastefully — as warmth, not caricature. Direct and fun. Not corporate, not formal German.

**Personality (3–5 adjectives):** Playful · Warm · Bold · Cool · Fiery

**Examples of voice:**
- ✅ "Jerk wrap. Eight-fifty. You'll be back tomorrow."
- ✅ "Pick your heat: mild, medium, or full fire 🔥"
- ✅ "New on Kotti Damm. From the Ssam team. One love."
- ❌ "Experience the authentic taste of Jamaica in the heart of Berlin" (too corporate)
- ❌ "We are pleased to announce our grand opening" (too formal)
