# Referral & Loyalty Plan — What a Jerk

*QSR-grade loyalty. No app required. Low-friction, physical-first, Ssam cross-promo built in.*

---

## Why This Works for a Restaurant

Unlike SaaS, a restaurant can't drop a "refer a friend" widget into its software. But food has its own viral physics:
- **The plate is the product.** People Instagram their food without being asked.
- **The meal is the trigger.** The best "aha moment" happens 10 minutes into eating.
- **Frequency is the goal.** A döner regular eats there 50x/year. You need to hit 20x to win.

So the loyalty plan has three layers:
1. **The punchcard** — turn one-timers into regulars (retention, not referral)
2. **"Bring a Friend"** — turn regulars into referrers (classic word-of-mouth)
3. **Ssam × What a Jerk cross-promo** — unlock an existing audience on day one
4. **UGC tag-to-win** — convert Instagram photos into free marketing

## Program 1 — The Jerk Punchcard

Classic, proven, zero-tech. Every customer gets a paper punchcard at the counter. After 5 purchases, they get a free wrap.

### Design

- **Card stock:** 2 × 4 inches (standard business card). Front: brand red `#CC0605` (RAL 3020) with yellow logo. Back: off-white `#E7EBDA` (RAL 9002) with black text. See [../brand/brand-guide.md](../brand/brand-guide.md).
- **Front:**
  ```
  WHAT A JERK
  Every 5th wrap free.

  [🔥] [🔥] [🔥] [🔥] [🔥]
  ```
  Stamp or hole-punch one 🔥 slot per purchase.
- **Back:**
  ```
  Kottbusser Damm 96 · 10967 Berlin
  Mon–Sun 12:00 · Fri/Sat till 01:00
  @whatajerkberlin · whatajerk.berlin

  Card is personal. Lose it, lose it.
  Expires: 6 months after first stamp.
  ```

### Rules

- Applies to wraps only (not dishes — protects margin on high-ticket items)
- One stamp per visit (no stacking multiple wraps into stamps)
- Free wrap = value up to €8.50 (any wrap on the menu)
- Collect the completed card when redeemed (gets it out of circulation)
- Staff stamps with a branded stamper → €30 one-time cost

### Expected impact

| Metric | Assumption | Expected |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Punchcards issued in month 1 | 600 (~20/day × 30 days) | — |
| Return rate (stamps 2+) | 30% | 180 |
| Completion rate (stamp 5 → free wrap) | 20% of returners | 36 free wraps |
| Cost of reward | 36 × €2.50 COGS | €90 |
| Incremental revenue from return visits | 180 × avg €15 × ~3 visits | €8,100+ |

Net: ~€90 cost, thousands in incremental revenue. **This is the best €90 you'll spend.**

### Digital alternative (V2, month 3+)

If you want a digital version:
- **Loyverse** POS has built-in loyalty (points per € spent)
- **Square Loyalty** similar
- **Stamp me** — simple SaaS, €10/month, QR-based digital stamp card
Skip for now. The physical card is warmer + cheaper.

## Program 2 — "Bring a Friend" Wednesday

Wednesdays are traditionally a restaurant's slow mid-week day. Use a referral mechanic to juice them.

### Mechanic

> **"Bring a Friend Wednesday" — buy one wrap, get a second for 50% off.**
> *Every Wednesday. Walk-in only. Not valid with punchcard.*

### Why it works

- Single-sided discount (no complex attribution)
- "Bring a friend" frames the discount as a social act, not a deal-chase
- 50% off second wrap preserves margin (customer still pays €12.75 for two wraps instead of €17)
- Drives two people in; the second person is likely new to the brand
- Doesn't devalue full-price days (only Wednesdays)

### Signage

**A-frame outside:**
```
BRING A FRIEND WEDNESDAY
Two wraps. Half off the second.
Every Wednesday. Walk-in only.
```

### Alternative: Referral card (higher friction, higher trust)

If the Wednesday deal doesn't move the needle:

- Give every customer a "Bring a Friend" card at checkout
- Friend brings card: both get a free patty
- Original customer must be present (ensures real referral)

Skip initially — punchcard + Wednesday promo is enough.

## Program 3 — Ssam × What a Jerk Cross-Promo

You already own the best audience in Berlin for this: Ssam's existing customers. Cross-promote on day one.

### Mechanic (opening week + ongoing)

**For Ssam customers:**
> "Show your Ssam receipt at What a Jerk this week → get 10% off your order. Valid through [opening week end date]."

**For What a Jerk customers:**
> "Show your What a Jerk receipt at Ssam → get 10% off your next Ssam visit."

### Why it works

- Ssam's 25–40 Neukölln demographic is an *exact match* for What a Jerk's target
- Receipts are authentic (can't fake), no QR/app required
- Creates a "restaurant family" story — you're not just opening another QSR, you're expanding a local hospitality footprint
- Reciprocal drives Ssam revenue too — legit win-win

### How to deploy

**Week -1 (May 8–13):**
- [ ] Print a small flyer (A6) with opening-week details, drop one in every Ssam takeaway bag
- [ ] IG cross-post on @ssam (and vice versa on @whatajerkberlin):
  > "Meet our new sister — What a Jerk. Opening Thursday May 14 on Kotti Damm. Show a Ssam receipt opening week for 10% off 🇯🇲"
- [ ] Email blast to Ssam customer list: "Something new on Kotti Damm"

**Opening week (May 14–20):**
- [ ] Train counter staff to ask: "Got a Ssam receipt? 10% off."
- [ ] Place a small table tent at Ssam: "Our sister opened this week → Kotti Damm 96"

**Ongoing (after opening week):**
- Convert 10% discount to a permanent 5% cross-promo (both directions)
- Or launch an actual combined loyalty program at 6 months (see V2 below)

### Staff script

Counter: "You got a Ssam receipt? I can take 10% off for you. Same team runs both."

This is the best sentence a staff member can say to a new customer in opening week. It's not a discount play — it's a **trust transfer** from a known-good brand to the new brand.

## Program 4 — UGC Tag-to-Win

Turn every customer's Instagram post into earned media.

### Mechanic

> **Tag @whatajerkberlin in a photo or Story. We'll repost our favorites + DM you a code for a free patty on your next visit.**

### Rules

- One free patty per person per month (prevent abuse)
- Must tag + photo must be of the food/place (not generic selfies)
- Staff or owner DM's a code (no app needed — just a 6-digit code written in marker on a napkin held up to camera if you want to be photogenic about it)
- Patty code redeemed in-person only

### Why it works

- IG's algorithm rewards user-generated content more than brand content — more reach
- The "DM us a code" mechanic creates a 1:1 conversation, which IG's algo surfaces
- A free patty costs ~€1 COGS — cheaper than any IG ad
- Reposted UGC fills your feed when you're low on content

### Month 1 target

- 30+ tagged posts
- 10 reposted to your grid/stories per week
- Response rate from DMs > 80%

### Signage (small table tent or receipt footer)

```
Tag @whatajerkberlin
Free patty incoming 🔥
```

## Program 5 — "Opening Week Heat Challenge" (one-off)

Launch-week buzz-builder. Instagram-friendly. Earns UGC.

### Mechanic

> **The Full Fire Challenge — order the 🔥🔥🔥🔥 "Full Fire" jerk wrap, finish it in one sitting, take a photo, tag us. Your photo goes on our wall. Your next wrap is free.**

### Why it works

- Creates a visual challenge (perfect for Reels)
- Self-selects adventurous eaters (your core demographic)
- "Wall of Fire" signage grows week by week → social proof artifact
- Keeps customers talking for weeks after
- Free 2nd wrap cost (€2.50 COGS) is cheap marketing

### Setup

- A small section of one wall dedicated to challengers' photos (polaroid-style prints or just IG screenshots)
- Small sign: "The Wall of Full Fire"
- Track names manually in a notebook
- Limit: one free wrap per person ever (prevents abuse)

### Opening week

- Post about the challenge on day 1
- Feature the first 5 completers in Stories
- End of week: "12 Full Fires, 0 casualties" post on feed

## Program 6 — Catering / Private Events (B2B tier, Month 3+)

Berlin offices, agency parties, small production companies are always looking for lunch catering. This is where referrals from one office can bring in 20 meals at a time.

### Mechanic (Month 3+ only, not launch priority)

- Simple catering menu: jerk chicken platter for 10 people, curry goat platter, patty box
- Minimum order €150, 24 hour notice
- Referral incentive: **"Refer a catering client that books → we send you a €50 voucher for your next visit."**

### Skip until

- Month 3+ (when ops are stable and you have bandwidth)
- You have at least 3 catering inquiries organically (signal demand exists)

## Metrics to Track

### Daily (5 min end-of-shift)

- Punchcards issued today (tally mark in till drawer)
- Punchcards redeemed today
- Ssam receipts honored today

### Weekly

- Punchcard issuance rate (target: 30%+ of customers take one)
- Punchcard redemption rate (target: 15% of issued get to 5 stamps)
- Wednesday "Bring a Friend" sales lift vs. prior Wednesday
- UGC tags received

### Monthly

- Estimated repeat customer rate (rough proxy: punchcard redemptions / customer count)
- Ssam cross-promo volume
- "Wall of Full Fire" entries
- IG-driven free patties redeemed (DM code entries)

## Anti-Abuse Rules

- Punchcard: collect when redeemed, one stamp per visit per customer
- UGC free patty: once per customer per month, manual code tracking
- Ssam cross-promo: physical receipt only, dated within 30 days
- Full Fire Challenge: photo proof required, one per customer ever
- Bring a Friend Wednesday: both people must be present at checkout

Keep it warm but enforced. One abuse case shouldn't make you tighten rules for everyone — just call it out with that customer privately.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Don't make the punchcard too hard to fill.** 10 stamps is too long — 5 is perfect for monthly ritual.
- **Don't launch a "premium" loyalty tier opening month.** Focus on one simple card. Tiers come later.
- **Don't mix discounts.** Punchcard ≠ Wednesday ≠ Ssam cross-promo. Each customer picks one offer per visit.
- **Don't give away dishes, only wraps.** Dishes are your margin protection.
- **Don't have staff discount "because they seem nice."** Discipline around promos is what makes them feel special.
- **Don't build an app.** 99% of restaurant loyalty apps fail. Paper works.
- **Don't run all these programs at once on day 1.** Launch punchcard + Ssam + UGC in week 1. Add Wednesday + Full Fire in week 2. Don't overload customers or staff.
