# Social Content Plan — What a Jerk

*Primary: Instagram. Secondary: TikTok. Tertiary: Threads (cross-post only).*
*Horizon: Week -3 through Month 2.*
*Goal: 1,000+ IG followers by end of Month 1; drive walk-ins + Wolt orders.*

---

## Content Pillars

| Pillar | % of feed | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Food hero shots** | 40% | Make people hungry. Mouth-watering product shots. |
| **Behind the kitchen** | 20% | Prove the food is real — grill, marinade, prep. |
| **Story + people** | 15% | Ethan's Vancouver/Toronto origin, staff intros, the Ssam connection. |
| **Neighborhood** | 15% | Kotti Damm love — regulars, late-night crowd, the street itself. |
| **Education / fun** | 10% | "What's a scotch bonnet?" "How spicy is Jerk?" explainers + jokes. |

*Do not post menus as static graphics. Dead content. Instead show food being made + eaten.*

---

## Format Split (Instagram)

- **Reels**: 40% (algorithm priority — highest reach)
- **Carousels**: 30% (saves + shares)
- **Single photo**: 20% (mouth-watering hero shots)
- **Stories**: daily, 3–10 per day

---

## Voice Reminder

Playful, welcoming, cool. Bilingual (lead EN, sprinkle DE). Use "ya mon" / "one love" *sparingly* — once a week, not every post. Never use exclamation points more than once per caption. Never use "exotic" or "ethnic." Never use corporate restaurant-speak.

**Good caption examples:**
- "Soft wrap. Smoky jerk. Slaw. That's it. €8.50. 🔥"
- "Ssam's sister just arrived on Kotti Damm. One love."
- "Festival is what happens when cornmeal, sugar, and frying oil agree on everything. €3 side. Worth it."

**Bad caption examples:**
- "Experience the authentic taste of Jamaica!! 🇯🇲🇯🇲🇯🇲" (cringe)
- "We are thrilled to welcome you to our grand opening." (corporate)
- "Hand-crafted jerk marinade made with love." (fake heritage)

## Content Production Brief (for photo/video shoots)

### Hero food shots (shoot #1 and #2)

**Equipment:** natural daylight near window OR warm softbox. 50mm lens or phone portrait mode.

**Shot list — absolute must-haves:**
1. Jerk chicken wrap — whole, shot on dark rustic board, jerk aioli dripping visible
2. Jerk chicken wrap — unwrapped/cut in half, slaw + chicken exposed
3. Oxtail stew — top-down on brand red `#CC0605` surface (tray, placemat, or tablecloth), steam visible
4. Curry goat — side angle, sauce pooling, rice & peas beside
5. Patty — cut in half, steam rising, golden-brown crust
6. Plate of 3 patties — overhead, diagonal composition
7. Escovitch side — close-up, peppers + onion glistening
8. Festival — golden-fried, torn in half
9. Counter top-down — full menu spread, overhead shot

**Style:**
- Warm golden tones (not cold/white)
- Bright but dramatic — shadows are OK
- NO over-styled food-magazine tropes (crossed chopsticks, fake smoke)
- Real sides, real portion sizes
- Brand color accents in plating surface and styling props: brand red `#CC0605`, yellow `#EFA94A`, with occasional blue `#5D9B9B` or pink `#EA899A` for variety. See [../brand/brand-guide.md](../brand/brand-guide.md).

### Reel-specific shot list

1. Jerk chicken going on grill — close-up, smoke
2. Marinade being brushed on raw chicken — slow-mo drip
3. Wrap being wrapped — hands in frame, paper crinkle, finished wrap wrapped in foil
4. Plate leaving the pass — "hands" holding plate
5. First customer walking in, big grin
6. Owner voice-over moments (shot on phone, natural audio)
7. Kotti Damm exterior wide shot — dusk light, signage lit
8. Late-night shot — Fri 22:00, crowd inside, warm interior vs dark street

### Photography budget
If hiring: ~€500–800 for a 4-hour food photographer (Berlin rates for a restaurant shoot). Alternative: phone + natural light + editing in Lightroom Mobile is viable for 80% of content. The key investment is **food styling**, not the camera.

## TikTok Strategy (secondary channel)

**Frequency:** 3x/week minimum (Mon/Wed/Fri), bonus 1x/weekend.

**What works on TikTok for restaurants:**
- POV walk-in: "POV: you walked into Berlin's first Jamaican QSR"
- ASMR cooking (grill sounds, chopping)
- "Rate my Berlin lunch" / trend-jacking
- Staff answering "what's jerk?" to a camera
- Patty pull-apart (classic format, always works)

**Core TikTok reels (first 30 days):**
1. Opening day vlog (60 sec)
2. "What is jerk chicken?" explainer (owner POV, 45 sec)
3. Patty pull-apart ASMR (15 sec)
4. Grill session time-lapse (30 sec, reggae music)
5. Owner's Vancouver-to-Berlin story (60 sec — repurpose IG reel)
6. Late-night wrap at 1am ("Berlin's only 1am jerk")
7. "Heat level test" with Berlin friends

**Rule:** Cross-post TikToks to IG Reels always. Never cross-post IG Reels to TikTok without re-editing (TikTok audience punishes obvious cross-posts).

## Never Post

- Staff complaints / customer complaints
- Discount-heavy promos (once a month max — devalues brand)
- Dog/pet photos unless it's a customer's dog (and customer is present)
- Political content
- Anything that'd embarrass Ssam (you're associated; keep both brands dignified)
- Food that looks bad — if a shot isn't beautiful, don't post it
